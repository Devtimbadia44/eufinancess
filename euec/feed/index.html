What Elliot Found

According to his findings, the market’s randomness and chaos, actually had repeating cycles. He pointed out that they were emotions of investors caused by influences from outside the market itself. Nowadays, external influences are Bloomberg, CNBC, ESPN, and others.

The predominant psychological frame of mind at the time of these seemingly random fluctuations was responsible for the order he saw.

Understanding Fractals Before Elliott Wave

Before we get into the whole trading with Elliott wave thing, let us look at something significant in this topic. You will have to know what fractals are first. In short, fractals are structures that can be split into parts, each of which is very similar to a copy of the whole thing.

This is a property that mathematicians call self-similarity. It is the same property that makes snowflakes look like they have smaller copies of themselves within themselves, the more you zoom in. Shells, clouds, and lightning bolts are all fractals.

So, where do they factor into this? Well, one of the properties of the Elliott waves is that they are fractals. Like clouds and seashells, they can be subdivided into smaller Elliott wave, very similar to the overarching whole.

The Three Cardinal Rules Governing The Elliott Wave Theory

Trading with Elliott wave is all about being able to accurately and correctly identify the waves. You need the right eye and three rules, which you can never break. They will help you label waves.

Here they are:

  1. Wave 3 can never be the shortest impulse wave
  2. Wave 2 can never go beyond the beginning of wave 1
  3. Wave 4 can never cross in the same price are that wave 1 is in

There it is. If you know these, you can identify the parts of the wave and, in that way, know what you are looking at.

And now, the Elliott wave trading guidelines. Unlike the cardinal rules, these are not absolute:

  1. Wave 5 does not move beyond the end of wave 3. We call this truncation
  2. Wave 5 often goes beyond the trend line drawn from wave 3, parallel to a trend line that connects the start of wave three and wave 5.
  3. Wave 3 tends to be long, sharp and extended.
  4. Wave 2 and 4, often bounce off levels indicate by Fibonacci retracements.

You Wonder: What is 1% and 3% penetration, and how do we calculate them?

Let’s do that one. Let’s start with the 3% penetration math. The percentage references the extent to which the price moves beyond the support and resistance point. We express it as a percentage of the length of the candle.

Let’s say that a candle breaks the red support line closed at a price of 8073.50. The candle high could be at 8625.90. The whole candle moves from the high to the closing price, and the calculation is as follows:

8625.9 – 8073.5 = 552.4

So, we get the difference first. The support line is at 8289.98 in this case. The distance from the support line to the closing price is, therefore:

8289.98 – 8073.5= 216.48.

To get the penetration percentage, we do the calculation as follows:

[216.48/552.4] X 100% = 39%

So, the penetration here is 39%, and that is more than the 3% needed to say that a price breakout has happened. With this, you will be able to know when to trade a bounce situation with the Kumo top/bottom or see a breakout or a role reversal situation.

The Forex Indicators You Should Use for Forex and CFD Trading

1. Stochastics

Among forex indicators, we have those that we call oscillators. This is one of them. As a forex trader, you can use it in two ways:

  • It can be a way for you to spot when there are price extremes (like when the market is overbought or oversold)
  • You can also use it to detect the divergences.

These are the two main things that the oscillator will show you. As forex indicators go, this is a beneficial function that you cannot do without. When forex and CFD trading, you will need to know when long-term and short-term price reversals will happen.

You can use the stochastics in tandem with other forex indicators and, in that way, always be ahead of the curve. Price reversals always happen in the market. That is the reason why you will need this one among your forex indicators toolbox.

When a trend has run to almost the end of its course, you will need to know when the next price movement is coming.

Oscillators are the leading forex indicators when it comes to detecting that kind of thing. It does not matter if you are CFD trading or currency trading, you will always need to know when the next move is coming and how it is going to look like.

In our other articles, we have talked about leading indicators. Stochastics are leading forex indicators, which show trends before they happen. In forex and CFD trading, you will always need to know what pattern to expect next.

You will not have a hard time detecting the overbought and the oversold areas when you have stochastics forex indicators. It pairs very well with the Fibonacci retracement tool. You can use them to recognize the re-entries into the long-term trend after price retracement.

Last but not least, the divergence and extremes in prices, are usually accurate and can be converted into very profitable trends.

2. The Market Profile Indicator

This is one of those forex indicators used in forex and CFD trading. It can show you on the charts, where there is a maximum offer volume or maximum bid, for a currency pair or a CFD asset.

With the ability to see the price at which the institutional traders are buying and selling an asset in substantial volumes, you can then know at which price you need to be buying or selling an asset in CFD trading or forex.

This will ensure that you do not get stuck making lifeless trades that do not seem to go anywhere.

There are many sources of the market profile indicator. Some of them you do not need to pay for, but others require you to pay. The forex indicators are useful, and if you feel like paying will get you somewhere, then, by all means, buy one.

Sometimes, this forex indicator is called a volume profile indicator because you can see the volume profile for any asset (in the case of CFD trading) at any given time when the markets are live.

The advantage you get when using this market profile forex indicator when compared to other volume indicators is that it can plot volume on the Y-axis of price and not on the X-axis.

The reason why this is so important in forex and CFD trading is so that you have better volume information at every specific price level.

The movements and activities are all plotted out as things happen, and not after they have happened.

3. The Auto Pivot Point Calculator

This is another excellent forex indicator that you will need when doing forex and CFD trading. With it, you can derive pivot points on the chart from the previous day on closing, low, and high prices. Instead of crunching the numbers yourself (math is hard), you can just use this tool, and it will do that work for you automatically.

There are two versions of this forex indicator. One of them calculates the weekly pivot points and the common one that we are used to calculates the daily pivot points. We are going to focus on the one that calculates the daily pivot points.

When you are doing day trading on forex or CFD trading, you can be able to see the daily price levels and be able to find the support and resistance. Typically, we have three resistance levels and three support levels and then the central pivot.

Even though they are named like this, the reality is that the pivot levels above the market price can function as resistance, and the ones below the market price can work as the support.

You can use them individually and separate from each other, or you can combine them with different forex tools and indicators so that they produce entry and exit signals for your forex and CFD trading activities.

With a forex indicator like this, you can be very exact in what you do as they will show you where the price activity for intraday trading starts and where it stops. Using the pivot points, you can comfortably choose 3-5 setups on currency pairs and get at least 20 pips on each of them on a trading day.

4. Fibonacci Tools

Do not ever fail to use the Fibonacci tools in forex and CFD trading. You will deny yourself one of the most significant opportunities to make money in the world of forex. Fibonacci tools base their function on a sequence of numbers discovered and described by a mathematician named Fibonacci, from several centuries ago.

These numbers alert us to retracement and continuation points, which then correspond with wave sequences that you find in price actions, according to the definitions provided by Charles Dow and Elliot.

With the use of Fibonacci numbers, it is easy to trade the retracements, set prices to target re-entry for trades, and the Elliot sequences as well.

With the Fibonacci forex indicator, you can draw retracements at 23.6%, 38.3%, 50%, 61.8% and 78.6%. This will include swing lows to swing highs and the reverse when in a downtrend. As a trader doing forex and CFD trading, you will be able to have a clear idea where a retracement from a low or high that has happened, will end.

With this, you know where the re-entry opportunity will be on the long-term trend.

The Fibonacci expansion tool is something you can use to define price targets from re-entry trades that have a clear definition from the Fibonacci retracement tool. With something like this, you can avoid all the times when you give profits back to the market by leaving your retracement trades at the best time.
