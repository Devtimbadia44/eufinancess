Pivot Points Calculator

In forex, a pivot point is an indicator in technical analysis, calculations and is used in determining the overall trend of the market over different time frames. The pivot point is just the average of the high, low, and closing prices from the previous trading day.

The pivot calculator helps you in calculating the pivot points for forex and SBI FX, forex options, commodities, bonds, stocks, options, futures, options, and other investment securities with a high, low, and closing price, during a given period.

They can be used to measure market sentiment as well. As a trader, the pivot points calculator is a valuable tool.

Pip Value Calculator

Using this tool, a trader can know the actual value of a pip in the currency that they choose. Typically, all you need to do when using the calculator is:

  • Set the pip amount
  • Choose the currency pair for the trade
  • Select the number of lots you wish to trade
  • Choose the currency you want the pip value to be displayed in

The currency pair define the pip value that you are trading, the size of the trade, and the exchange rate of the pair. The pip value calculator will divide one pip (normally 0.0001) by the current market value of the forex pair.

Fibonacci Calculator

The Fibonacci levels are a concept in technical analysis. They are used as indicators of the forex exchange market. Traders use them to determine the points in the graph where the breakdowns and the rebounds will happen.

The Fibonacci calculator will let you know where this will happen when you are doing an analysis. All the information you will get goes to helping you set the stop-loss, the take-profit, at the Fibonacci levels. With that, your chances of success increase.

Position Size Calculator

The position size calculator will work when you enter the size of your trading account and the percentage of the account you are willing to risk on each trade. You will then choose the currency pair that you would like to trade, and the calculator will give you the appropriate position you need to go with for that particular trade.

Position size simply refers to the number of units you invest in a particular security. As always, you should take into account the size and the risk tolerance for appropriate position sizing. That is why the calculator is essential.

The Risk and Reward Calculator

As a trader, you will need to calculate the position’s best targets and their risk-to-reward ratios. The calculations done here are based on the Fibonacci retracements from the local peak and the bottom. A calculator is a handy tool that will help determine the potential risks before you enter any position.

When you start, you will calculate the Fibonacci retracements to form the entry, target, and stop-loss levels. It is usually wise to enter positions only when the current price is close to the Fibonacci level. You will reduce the risk significantly.

Gain and Loss Percentage Calculator

The calculator will quickly tell you what percentage of the account balance you have won or lost. You will also get an estimate of the percentage of the current balance that you will require to reach the breakeven point again.

The tool is excellent when you want to understand even better how much you have won or lost in your trade. If you had a losing trade, you would know what is needed to get back to breakeven. With a winning trade, you know how much you can afford to lose before you return to the starting point.

Elliott Wave Videos

The Elliott Wave principle proposes that the collective investor psychology or market sentiment oscillates between optimism and pessimism. All of this happens in natural sequences. The mood swings create patterns, of which price movements of the market are evidence.

At every degree of a trend of time scale, you will see the Elliott Wave. In trading, you can use the information coming from the wave patterns to know if the price movement is impulsive, or a retracement. You get to do all this before the price changes the overall structure as sentiment changes.

Interest Rate Table

The interest rate is the amount a lender will charge you for using his/her assets. An interest rate table is a tool you can use to break down the math and know what you have to do to pay back. The interest table expresses this as a percentage of the principal.

The interest tables are prepared in many forms. The variations are usually on the level of detail offered and refinement of things like the decimal places, time intervals, and other things that may or may not be useful to you. The tables allow you to stay ahead on payments and such.

Currency Strength Meter

The currency strength meter is a visual representation that guides you through the information regarding the strongest and weakest currencies at any given time. For instance, to know the strength of the US dollar, the currency strength meter will calculate the strength of all pairs that contain the USD.

The meter will then put these calculations together to determine how the dollar performs overall. The thing about measuring the currency strength meter is that you need the latest tech because older tech like MT4 might freeze, and your CPU will be forced to work at 100% continuously.

Forex Calendar

Also known as an economic calendar, a forex calendar is a valuable tool that tells you about upcoming events that can shape the fundamental analysis you may be doing for your trades. With it, you can get information about the GDP, the consumer price index, and the non-farm payroll report, among others.

There is a lot of central bank intervention happening in the world today, and with the fiscal cliffs thrown into the mix, you will need to have the information about when events take place that could change the market, so you are not caught off-guard.

Pip Calculator

In trading, a pip is a price in a given exchange rate. When you are a trader, you will need to know the change in the value of pips, which is where the pip calculator comes in. Without pips, you will not have reference to gains or losses.

The pip measures the amount of change you have in the exchange rate for a currency pair. This amount is calculated to the last decimal point. Because the significant currency pairs are priced to four decimal places, minimal changes are calculated using pips (the equivalent of 1/100 of 1%). Pip Calculator helps every trader to calculate this.

Forex Profit Calculator

This calculator is one of the best tools you can have when you want to stay on top of what you have gained and to see what you have lost. The profit calculator will let you know how much you have and how to make sure that you do not dip below your principal amount.

With a forex profit calculator, you will always have a way to know how much money is available and how much you can afford to lose when you are trading. Management of funds is one of the tenets every forex trader must learn to survive.
