What Is Forex Trading?

Also known as FX trading or Foreign Exchange, forex trading is the conversion of currencies from one to another. It is the most active market in the world and the most traded with a daily trading volume of about $5 trillion.

Within forex trading, we have a network of sellers and buyers who change currency between one another at a price. Companies, individuals and banks convert currency from one into another. If you travel, you are bound to do this.

A sizeable amount of money changing from one currency to another is people who are trying to get usable currency when in foreign countries but most of the time, it is about making profits.

The amount that gets converted daily can affect the stability of some currencies. This kind of volatility on the currency is the reason why traders in currency exist with the sole purpose of making profits. The volatility can lead to profits but also risks at the same time.

Forex trading involved people who know how these currencies work and watching for them to change when compared to other currencies and the selling or buying, depending on what will net them the highest profit.

That is the simplest way to put it. For more details, you can look at the forex education section where we have eleven levels worth of topics to cover on what the whole thing entails.

What Are the Different Types of Forex Brokers?

In the forex market, we have different kinds of brokers that you can choose from. You will need a forex broker to handle the forex transactions that you will be making. This is an important decision and part of the decision requires looking through forex brokers reviews to find out which ones are good for you.

We have three main types you will find when looking through forex reviews and we will look at each of them briefly.

  • Market Makers
  • No-Dealing Desk
  • Electronic Communications Network

Each of them has a unique way of handling the transactions that you will be making and executing orders. What makes them different from one another if the premise for forex trading is the same?

Well, let’s briefly look at that.

1. Market Makers

With a market maker, you will get offered two-sided markets from a specialist broker who is a part of the in-house dealing desk. If the price is agreed upon, the broker will take the other side of any of the transactions from the client.

They will buy their bid side or selling on the offer side of the price that was quoted at the beginning of the deal.

In simple terms, the goal here is to cover the spread and to get substantial volume on both sides of the market and in that way to avoid being forced to lay off the risk accumulated with a professional counterparty.

If the trade is large, the market maker could choose to offset the trade instantaneously, more so if the market maker thinks that the market might move against it. They could also include it in their trading book, depending on the outlook of the market and the transaction size.

2. No Dealing Desk Brokers

From the name, you can tell that they do not pass their client’s orders through a dealing desk. That means they do not take your side of the trade but they link two parties together. They act as a bridge that will get you over impassable terrain.

This way, they will connect you to the people that you will be dealing with to make profits. They could be banks, hedge funds, mutual funds, other brokers, etc.

The No Dealing Desk types charge a small commission or adjust the spread a little bit by putting a markup on it.

3. Electronic Communications Network Brokers

They do not have a dealing desk generally. The brokers give you an electronic trading platform where pro-market-makers, traders and others in the market enter bids and offer using their electronic platforms.

The thing is that the trader who is posting the price will be given anonymity. This is great for traders who are on the bid or offer and do not want to disclose their identity.

If the terminology sounds a bit complicated, then you would be very happy to learn that the forex education covers such things if you are interested.
